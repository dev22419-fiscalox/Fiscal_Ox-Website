Journal Voucher Entries

Accounting Oct 21, 2021

Journal voucher (JV) can be a little confusing sometimes for accounting students and most of the times they face a little difficulty in creating Journal vouchers and general journal entries.

General journal is a term used for book of original entry in which all the business transactions are recorded at first time. Journal voucher refers to a document that posts or records a transaction in the general journal. Journal voucher entries are sometimes referred to all those entries which are other than the routine transactions like entries of account payable or account receivable. In current accounting systems where the transactions are systemized, journal vouchers are used to rectify any error or wrong posting. Sometimes, journal voucher entry is used to enter such transactions which can’t be supported by third party documents like invoice or any other supporting document.

Journal voucher entries are generally prepared for the following transactions:

  • To record depreciation
  • Adjustment or rectification entries
  • Opening entries
  • Closing entries
  • Transfer of balance from one account to another
  • Accrual expenses or income
  • Sales or purchase of fixed assets

Elements of Journal Voucher Entry
Journal voucher is piece of paper which contains all the necessary information about the entry and holds proper signature from authorized persons. A journal voucher should contain the following information:

  • Name of the office or department
  • A unique identification number
  • Date on which this document is produced
  • Clear description about the entry
  • Debit and credit account information
  • Amount must be written in both figures and words
  • Proper signature of the person who prepares the voucher
  • Proper signature from approved authority

Common rules for preparing Journal Voucher

  • For each transaction there should be a separate journal voucher used.
  • The person who prepares the entry should have proper knowledge of double entry book keeping.
  • All the supporting documents and signatures should be attached with the JV.
  • All the narration should be clearly written.
