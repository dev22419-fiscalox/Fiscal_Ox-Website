Fiscal Ox at ITM Vadodara – Inspiring Innovation, One Student at a Time

The team at Fiscal Ox was delighted to visit ITM Vadodara for an engaging campus interaction session with the bright minds of the university’s computer science and IT departments. The session served not only as a recruitment and training initiative but also as a platform to inspire students to think beyond conventional boundaries and imagine themselves as future innovators and entrepreneurs.

Co-founders Vicrum Vuppalapaty and Lekshmi Nair led an impactful discussion on how cloud computing is shaping global industries, and why the Indian market is now poised to harness its full potential. The session also featured live coding challenges, with prizes awarded to students who successfully solved real-world problem statements—bridging the gap between theory and practice.

Key highlights:

  • Conducted an interactive and inspiring placement-focused session with students from Computer Science and IT departments.
  • Vicrum Vuppalapaty and Lekshmi Nair shared valuable insights on cloud computing, entrepreneurship, and building global-standard tech for India.
  • Discussed how cloud technology is transforming industries globally and how India is now positioned to fully leverage its potential.
  • Engaged students through real-world problem-solving tasks with rewards—bridging academic learning and practical application.
  • Students asked deep, thoughtful questions about startup journeys, funding, career paths, and product development.
  • Shared their inspiring story of launching Fiscal Ox from scratch—without legacy or financial support, driven by purpose and passion.
  • Highlighted the company’s mission to provide cloud-first, user-friendly solutions for India’s small and mid-sized businesses, replacing outdated desktop tools.
  • The session left students motivated and energized, with a clearer vision of how they can become innovators and tech leaders.
  • Fiscal Ox expressed sincere thanks to ITM faculty and students for their engagement and reaffirmed its commitment to mentoring young talent.

The discussion quickly turned interactive, with students asking thoughtful and bold questions. They were curious to know how Fiscal Ox began—how the founders secured funding, made early decisions, and what motivated them to leave comfort zones and build something of their own. Vicrum and Lekshmi shared their honest journey: without any legacy, financial backing, or established network, they began their careers in the U.S., saving diligently while gaining valuable industry experience. With a deep passion for innovation and a clear vision, they returned to build products tailored for India, engineered to global standards.

At the heart of their motivation was one shared belief: small businesses deserve access to world-class technology. While big tech companies typically design enterprise solutions for large corporations, Fiscal Ox is solving the problems that small and mid-sized Indian businesses face every day—replacing outdated desktop tools with intuitive, affordable, cloud-first solutions that bring efficiency, security, and scalability.

The session left students not just informed, but inspired—energized by the possibility of building meaningful, accessible technology from the ground up. Fiscal Ox extends its sincere thanks to the faculty and students of ITM Vadodara for their warmth, enthusiasm, and meaningful engagement. We're excited about the future these young innovators are shaping—and proud to support them along the way.
