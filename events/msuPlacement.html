Fiscal Ox at the Department of Computer Applications, Faculty of Science, The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda

Fiscal Ox was honored to be invited to the Department of Computer Applications, Faculty of Science, The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda, for a campus placement initiative that blended technical insight with hands-on engagement. Our co-founders, Vicrum Vuppalapaty and Lekshmi Nair, led a dynamic session that offered students a unique look into how cloud-based technologies are reshaping the global business landscape.

The event centered on the real-world impact of cloud computing, showcasing how industries—especially in the U.S.—have embraced cloud infrastructure to simplify processes, enhance security, and drive innovation. The discussion covered how SaaS platforms are not just tools for efficiency but enablers of business transformation for both small and large enterprises.

Key highlights:

  • Fiscal Ox was invited to MSU Baroda to engage with students through a blend of technical discussions and interactive learning experiences.
  • Co-founders Vicrum Vuppalapaty and Lekshmi Nair led the session, offering firsthand industry insights and inspiration.
  • Explored how cloud-based technologies and SaaS platforms are revolutionizing industries—especially in the U.S.—by enhancing security, streamlining operations, and driving innovation.
  • The session featured live coding challenges and interactive games, helping students connect academic knowledge with real-world problem-solving.
  • Students actively participated, asking insightful questions about product innovation, entrepreneurship, and career opportunities in tech.
  • Vicrum and Lekshmi shared their startup story—from building Fiscal Ox without a legacy or financial backing to creating cloud-first solutions tailored for Indian businesses.
  • Emphasized the company’s commitment to empowering small and mid-sized Indian businesses with intuitive, secure, and scalable technology.

To keep the session highly engaging and educational, Vicrum introduced coding challenges and games that encouraged students to actively participate. These real-time problem-solving exercises helped students make meaningful connections between their academic concepts and the actual software development lifecycle. Through these challenges, students clearly saw how textbook fundamentals translate into powerful real-world tools. Students showed tremendous curiosity, asking thoughtful questions about product innovation, cloud adoption, and their future roles in the evolving tech ecosystem. Vicrum and Lekshmi shared insights into how Fiscal Ox is building one-of-a-kind, customer-centric cloud solutions that solve age-old problems posed by legacy desktop applications—solutions designed not just for functionality but for intuitive, modern user experience tailored to India’s unique business landscape.

We extend our heartfelt thanks to the faculty and students of MSU Baroda for their warm welcome and inspiring engagement. Fiscal Ox remains committed to mentoring young talent and building transformative technology that bridges academia and industry while redefining what’s possible through the cloud.
